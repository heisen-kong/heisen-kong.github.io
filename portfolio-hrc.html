Exploring Human & Robot Co-design Methods

Human-Robot Interaction • Robotics • Research

Collaborative Design Platform for Humans and Robots

Developed a collaborative design platform where humans and robots co-create layouts together, exploring new interaction paradigms in creative work.

The goal was to investigate how gesture-based interfaces and robotic manipulation can enable seamless collaboration between humans and robots in design tasks, creating a more intuitive and productive co-creation experience.

Research Approach

A Novel Interface for Co-creation

Working with the hrc² lab at Cornell, we developed an interactive platform that enables humans to communicate design intent through natural hand gestures, which the robot interprets and executes through precise manipulation.

The system combines computer vision, gesture recognition, and robotic control to create a seamless feedback loop where both human and robot contribute their unique strengths to the creative process.

Technical Implementation

Gesture Recognition and Robotic Control

Built a gesture recognition system using OpenCV and MediaPipe to detect and interpret hand-drawn shapes in real-time. The system translates these gestures into actionable commands for the robotic arm.

Integrated with ROS (Robot Operating System) to enable a Kinova Gen3 robotic arm to execute precise pick-and-place tasks based on the detected gestures. The challenge was achieving low latency and high accuracy to maintain a natural interaction flow.

Research Outcomes

Insights on Human-Robot Collaboration

The research revealed key insights into how humans and robots can effectively collaborate on creative tasks. Participants found the gesture-based interface intuitive and appreciated the robot's ability to maintain precision while they focused on conceptual design.

The study demonstrated that combining human creativity with robotic precision creates opportunities for new forms of collaborative design work, with implications for manufacturing, education, and creative industries.

Impact

Advancing Human-Robot Interaction

This research contributes to the growing field of human-robot collaboration by demonstrating practical methods for co-design. The findings have implications for future interactive systems where humans and robots work together as creative partners.
